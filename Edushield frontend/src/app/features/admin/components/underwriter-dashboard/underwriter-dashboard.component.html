How Premiums Are Calculated

Premiums are dynamically adjusted based on the risk score. The risk score (0–100) is calculated from:

Parent Age (21–30: 10pts, 31–40: 15pts, 41–50: 25pts, 51–60: 40pts)
Occupation Risk (5–30pts based on profession)
Annual Income (>₹15L: 5pts, ₹8–15L: 10pts, ₹4–8L: 20pts, <₹4L: 30pts)
Coverage Amount (≤₹10L: 5pts, ₹10–25L: 10pts, ₹25–50L: 20pts, >₹50L: 30pts)

Risk Multipliers:

LOW: x1.0
MEDIUM: x1.2
HIGH: x1.5
V.HIGH: x2.0

Underwriter Guidelines

  • Verify identity documents match child details before approval.
  • Risk scores > 75 require careful secondary review.
  • You can revise the premium during approval based on your assessment.
  • Always provide a reason when rejecting an application.
  • Coverage must not exceed 5x the applicant's annual income.
